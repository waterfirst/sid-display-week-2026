Head-up Display Based on Micro-lens to Improve Micro-LED Brightness
Yantao Guan*, Guowei Zha*, Fancheng Liu*, Huanli Yang*, Jianfeng Yuan*, Jingfeng Xue*
*TCL China Star Optoelectronics Technology Co., Ltd., Wuhan, Hubei, China

Abstract

We have studied the technology of preparing micro-lens on
micro-LED display. Through appropriate pixel arrangement and
the design of each sub-pixel corresponding to each micro-lens, we
can narrow the near-Lambertian light distribution of micro-LED
and make the maximum brightness of the panel four times that
without micro-lens. Then, according to the misalignment
relationship between the center positions of the micro-lens and
micro-LED, the main viewing angle directions of different
positions on a display are different to meet the main viewing angle
requirements of AR-HUD for the panel. Finally, based on the
optical design, an AR-HUD with a large FOV of about 15.4Â°*7.7Â°
was prepared. The peak eye brightness is >18000nit, the NTSC
exceeds 110%, and the eye box brightness uniformity is >70%. It
fully meets the viewing angle required by the optical and
mechanical design, which greatly promotes the application scope
of Micro-LED.

1. Introduction

With the upgrade of smart cockpits, AR-HUD has become the
core interactive device to improve driving safety, and the market
size is increasing year by year [1-2]. The application of large-scale
AR-HUD will bring about technological updates and iterations.
The display effect of AR-HUD is obviously restricted by light
source technology. Current mainstream light sources such as LCD
have insufficient brightness, DLP has a complex structure, and
laser costs are high, making it difficult to balance strong light
adaptability, miniaturization, and energy
consumption
requirements. Micro-LED technology provides a solution to this
dilemma[3].
First of all, micro-LED can achieve very high brightness, can
resist sunlight interference, and has a high contrast, which
effectively solves the problem of graying of the display. Secondly,
micro-LED light source has a high color gamut, NTSC>110%.
During driving, the high-color gamut warning icon can attract the
driver's attention and provide safety guarantee. Finally, the long
life of the micro-LED display, wider operating temperature range
and better stability [3-4] are undoubtedly another better choice for
vehicle display. As the industry continues to upgrade to high
brightness, high safety, high stability, wide operating temperature
range, and full-scenario adaptability, Micro-LED has become a
key direction for breakthroughs in AR-HUD technology.
This research is based on LTPS glass-based Micro-LED. It mainly
completes the substrate pixel design and optical structure design
to increase the display brightness by 3 times, and the main
viewing angles are different at different panel positions. Through
process and process development such as driver debugging and
opto-mechanical design, an AR-HUD device using glass-based
micro-LED as the image source was successfully prepared. Its
FOV is as high as 15.4Â°*7.7Â°, NTSC>110%, and eye
brightness>18000nit.

3.1 Micro-lens preparation:
We prepared micro-lens by imprinting based on the 4.63-inch
panel. The difference between the actual effect and the design is
very small. The diameter of the actual test lens is about 42um and
the radius is about 22um. According to the actual test, the effect
of the actual preparation of the micro-lens is in line with the
design;

This test point is the center point of the panel. The main viewing
angle in the H direction is about -1.5Â°, and the main viewing angle
in the V direction is about 15Â°, which is consistent with the main
viewing angle design. After adding micro-lens, the performance is
about 4 times that without micro-lens, which is very different
from the simulation estimate. It can be seen from the figure that
the brightness without micro-lens changes very little with the
angle. After adding micro-lens, the light energy in the H direction
is mainly concentrated between -17Â°~14Â°, and the viewing angle
width is about 31Â°. The light energy in the V direction is mainly
concentrated between 2Â°~27Â°, and the viewing angle width is
about 25Â°. The actual viewing angle width exceeds the required
23Â° in the H direction and 7Â° in the V direction. This design is
actually to prevent alignment deviation when preparing the lens,
so the viewing angle range is intentionally expanded.
The simulation and actual brightness improvements are
comparable, but there are still some deviations in the overlap of
the light distribution curves. The main reason is that the surface
brightness of the micro-LED is uneven.

3.4 Optical system and display module:
This 4.63-inch (1280*720 pixels) full-color display module uses a
TFT glass-based substrate to integrate a Micro-LED array. It is
specially designed for automotive head-up display (AR-HUD)
applications. It runs at a 120Hz refresh rate and has a peak
brightness of up to 140,000nit, ensuring visibility in strong light
environments. Its color gamut reaches 110% (the traditional
display module is about 70% of the AR-HUD color gamut of
LCD), which enriches the display colors of AR-HUD. Its highpurity colors can better catch the eye when displaying navigation
and display warnings, adding a solid guarantee for driving safety.
Micro-LED's pixel-level light emission and 0 grayscale almost
black display mode increase display contrast;

4. Conclusion
In this study, by designing micro-lens, the near-Lambertian light
distribution of micro-LED is narrowed, achieving an effect of
increasing the brightness by about 3 times. And through the
deviation of the center alignment of the micro-lens and microLED, different main viewing angles at different positions of the
panel are achieved to meet the AR-HUD's requirements for panel
viewing angles. Optical simulation is used to design the specific
parameters of the micro-lens, and estimate the brightness
improvement and main viewing angle shift, and then verify the
simulation based on actual preparation.
The design and preparation of Micro-lens has successfully
improved the brightness of Micro-LED displays by about 4 times.
Using this technology, we successfully developed a glass-based
4.6-inch (1280*720 pixels) AR-HUD display module with ultrahigh brightness and 110% color gamut. Based on this highbrightness and high color gamut display module, we further
designed and developed an AR-HUD opto-mechanical system,
providing more valuable experience for glass-based micro-LED
applications in AR-HUD.
